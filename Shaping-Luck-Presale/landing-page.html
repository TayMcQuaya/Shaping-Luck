The Book

The luckiest people
aren't lucky

Some people seem to attract extraordinary opportunities — the right meeting, the right timing, the right introduction — while the rest of us chalk it up to chance.

They're not lucky. They've built systems — habits, environments, and networks — that make serendipity almost inevitable.

Shaping Luck is the first book to lay bare the mechanics of engineered serendipity. Drawing on decades of research, real-world case studies, and the lived experience of two people who've spent their careers at the intersection of human connection and exponential change.

Luck isn't what happens to you.
It's what you build around you.

Why certain people consistently find themselves in the right room at the right time — and the invisible structures that make it repeatable.

Scale Your Luck

How to move from isolated lucky breaks to a compounding system of serendipity that accelerates over time.
