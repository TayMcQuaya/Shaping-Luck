The Price-First Playbook

TRUST → REVEAL → PRICE ANCHOR → STACK → STACK → STACK → "ALL OF THIS FOR $400?!" → ACTION

Old playbook: hide the price, build tension, reveal at the end. This playbook: drop the price early, then make it feel like a steal by stacking value on top for 40 minutes straight.

Voice & Energy Notes

Salim's Energy

Warm, personal, slightly nervous (genuine, not performed). He's showing something he cares about. Talks like he's telling a friend, not pitching an audience. When he drops the price at minute 25, it should feel casual and confident — "let me just be upfront with you."

John's Energy

Measured, precise, intellectual. The anchor. When Salim gets excited, John grounds it in research. He doesn't sell — he explains. That IS his sell. John's validation of the test runs carries more weight than any testimonial.

The Handoffs

Salim → John should feel like a conversation, not a relay race. They riff off each other. "John, tell them about..." / "Salim mentioned something earlier that I want to build on..." The chemistry between them IS the product proof.

After the Price Drop

The energy should feel lighter, not heavier. The price is out. No more elephant in the room. Now it's just "let me keep showing you cool stuff" — and the audience is along for the ride knowing exactly what it costs.
