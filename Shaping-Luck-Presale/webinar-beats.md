# Phase 1 Webinar — Beat Sheet

> April 1–2, 2026. Salim + John live together for the first time.

**Format:** Zoom webinar
**Hosts:** Salim Ismail + John Hagel
**Goal:** Reveal the app, drop $400 early, stack value on top until $400 feels absurd, close founding members live

---

## The Emotional Arc

```
TRUST → REVEAL → PRICE ANCHOR → STACK → STACK → STACK → "ALL OF THIS FOR $400?!" → ACTION
```

Old playbook: hide the price, build tension, reveal at the end.
This playbook: **drop the price early, then make it feel like a steal** by stacking value on top for 40 minutes straight.

---

## The Core Frame

The app has already been validated. 10+ internal test runs with OpenEXO Pro Elite members — real people, real connections, real results. This isn't a beta. This is a proven system expanding to a larger group.

Salim and John aren't asking the audience to take a leap of faith. They're inviting them into something that already works.

## Minute-by-Minute Beats

### ACT 1: CONNECTION (0:00 – 20:00)

---

**[0:00 – 3:00] THE OPEN — Salim**
_Beat: Warmth + vulnerability_

- "Hey everyone. I'm a little nervous today."
- Acknowledge the room — "You're here because you got in early. That matters."

_Beat: Origin story + personal stake_

- The personal story behind Shaping Luck
- "I used to call it luck. Now I'd call it inevitable — because I'd accidentally built the conditions that made it almost certain to happen. I just didn't have the language for it."
- The observation that kept coming up: the "luckiest" people all share invisible patterns

---

**[8:00 – 15:00] THE FRAMEWORK — John**
_Beat: Intellectual credibility + "aha" moment_

> **Internal note:** This is onboarding question #7 — plants the seed for the app reveal later. The audience is already doing the exercise without knowing it.

---

**[15:00 – 20:00] THE EXPERIMENT — John → Salim**
_Beat: Proof it already works_

### ACT 2: THE REVEAL + PRICE DROP (20:00 – 30:00)

---

> **Internal note:** This is the key structural move. The price is out. No more tension about "what's it going to cost." From this point forward, every new thing they see makes $400 feel like more of a steal. The audience spends the next 40 minutes thinking "and I get THAT too?!" instead of "when are they going to ask for money?"

| Already have the book?       | Don't have the book?         |

> **Internal note:** Links are live from minute 30 onward. Some people will buy immediately. Most will wait. That's fine — the next 40 minutes is designed to make them feel increasingly stupid for NOT buying.

Every segment from here adds value on top of the $400 they already know about. The audience keeps recalculating: "Wait, this too? For $400?"

### ACT 4: CLOSE (75:00 – 90:00)

> **Internal note:** Q&A is NOT filler. It's the second conversion window. Many people buy during Q&A after hearing others' questions validate their own interest. With price already out, Q&A questions tend to be "how do I buy?" and "when does it start?" rather than "what does it cost?" — much better energy.

## Why Price First Works Better

| Price-last (old approach)                                   | Price-first (this approach)                                        |
| ----------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| Audience spends 60 min wondering "what's it going to cost?" | Audience spends 40 min thinking "I can't believe all this is $400" |
| Tension builds but so does skepticism                       | Value stacks but price stays anchored                              |
| Price drop is a single moment — hit or miss                 | Every new feature is a micro-conversion moment                     |
| Q&A is full of "what does it cost?" questions               | Q&A is full of "how do I sign up?" questions                       |
| Feels like a pitch                                          | Feels like generosity                                              |

## Voice Notes

**Salim's energy:** Warm, personal, slightly nervous (genuine, not performed). He's showing something he cares about. Talks like he's telling a friend, not pitching an audience. When he drops the price at minute 25, it should feel casual and confident — "let me just be upfront with you" — not like a reveal.

**John's energy:** Measured, precise, intellectual. The anchor. When Salim gets excited, John grounds it in research. When the audience is skeptical, John's credibility absorbs it. He doesn't sell — he explains. That IS his sell. John's validation of the test runs ("this happened consistently") carries more weight than any testimonial.

**The handoffs:** Salim → John should feel like a conversation, not a relay race. They riff off each other. "John, tell them about..." / "Salim mentioned something earlier that I want to build on..." The chemistry between them IS the product proof — if these two can create serendipity together, so can you.

**After the price drop:** The energy should feel lighter, not heavier. The price is out. No more elephant in the room. Now it's just "let me keep showing you cool stuff" — and the audience is along for the ride knowing exactly what it costs.

---

## The One Rule

> The $400 price is stated early and never apologized for. Everything after that moment makes it feel like a bargain. By the time Salim recaps at minute 65, the audience isn't wondering IF they should buy — they're wondering why it's so cheap.
